# 30-Day Pinterest Content Calendar

**Cadence:** 5-7 fresh pins/day, posted via Pinterest's built-in scheduler.
**Posting times:** weekdays 8-11 PM ET, weekends 8-11 AM ET.
**Workflow:** batch on Sundays, top up Wednesdays.

Each row = one pin. Rotate niches so no single board gets >2 pins/day (anti-spam).

## Week 2 — Add 5 boards (7 pins/day)

Add: Fitness Gear, Best Photography, Laptops, Headphones & Audio, Personal Care.

## Week 3 — Add 5 boards (10 pins/day)

Add: Furniture, Pet Supplies, Home Office, Travel Accessories, Outdoor & Camping.

By now you should see your first 100+ impression pins. Identify them and create 3 variations of each.

---

## Week 4 — Optimization week

- Don't add new boards.
- Pinterest Analytics → "Pins" tab → sort by Impressions → take the top 5 pins, recreate each with:
  - New image (different angle/template)
  - New title (test different formula from PIN_DESCRIPTIONS.md)
  - Same destination URL
- Pin the variations to additional relevant boards (cross-pinning).
- Goal by end of month: 200+ pins live, 10k+ monthly viewers.

---

## Batch workflow (Sundays, ~90 min)

1. **0-15 min:** Open Pinterest Analytics → note top 3 performers from past week.
2. **15-45 min:** Generate 35 pin images (5/day × 7 days) using `scripts/banner_compose.py` or Canva.
3. **45-75 min:** Open Pinterest's scheduler → drag images, paste descriptions from your template bank, set times.
4. **75-90 min:** Schedule top-3 performers' variations across 2-3 boards each (cross-pin).

---

## What to track weekly

Open Pinterest Analytics → Overview, log these:

| Metric | Week 1 baseline | Goal Week 4 |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Monthly views | ~0 | 5,000-10,000 |
| Engaged audience | 0 | 200+ |
| Outbound clicks | 0 | 50+ |
| Top-performing pin (CTR) | n/a | identify it, scale it |
| Top-performing board | n/a | double down |

Cross-reference with Amazon Associates report → look for `ascsubtag=pin-*` rows to confirm Pinterest-driven sales.
