# Pin Descriptions + SEO Playbook

Pinterest is a visual search engine. Pins rank by **title + description + image alt text + board name** matching the user's query. Treat every pin like a mini blog post.

---

## The 5-part pin description formula

Use this skeleton for every pin. Total length: 400-500 chars (Pinterest allows 500).

```
[1. HOOK — emoji + benefit, 8-12 words]
[blank line]
[2. PRODUCT DESCRIPTION — what it is, 2 sentences]
[3. WHY IT'S GOOD — 2-3 specific features/benefits]
[4. CTA — "Tap to shop" / "Save for later"]
[5. KEYWORDS + DISCLOSURE — 3-5 hashtags + #affiliate]
```

### Worked example (Air Fryer pin):

> **Crispy fries in 12 minutes — no oil needed ✨**
>
> This 6-quart air fryer cooks family-size meals with 90% less oil than deep frying. Eight presets cover fries, wings, fish, veggies, and reheat — set it and forget it.
>
> Why it's worth it: dishwasher-safe basket, super quiet (under 65 dB), and Amazon's #1 air fryer for 2026. Under $80.
>
> Tap the pin to shop on Amazon →
>
> #airfryer #kitchengadgets #amazonfinds #healthycooking #affiliate

## Title formulas (40 char max — this is what shows in search)

Pinterest's title is the most-weighted search field. Front-load keywords.

### Formulas that rank:

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| `[Number] Best [Product] for [Audience]` | "5 Best Air Fryers for Small Kitchens" |
| `[Adjective] [Product] Under $[Price]` | "Quiet Robot Vacuums Under $300" |
| `[Year] [Product] [Use Case]` | "2026 Smart Plugs for Renters" |
| `How to [Goal] with [Product]` | "How to Whiten Teeth with [Product]" |
| `[Aesthetic] [Product] for [Space]` | "Cozy Reading Lamps for Bedrooms" |
| `[Product] That Actually [Verb]` | "Resistance Bands That Actually Last" |

### Avoid:

- ❌ "Cool kitchen stuff" (no keywords)
- ❌ "AMAZON DEAL!!! 🔥🔥🔥" (Pinterest deprioritizes shouty pins)
- ❌ "Click here →" (Pinterest doesn't allow link CTAs in titles since 2024)
- ❌ Brand-only titles ("Ninja Air Fryer") — add the long-tail context

## Hashtag strategy

Pinterest ranks 3-5 hashtags. More than 5 = spam signal. Always include:

1. **One niche-broad tag** (`#kitchengadgets`)
2. **One product-specific tag** (`#airfryer`)
3. **One audience tag** (`#smallkitchenhack`, `#busymom`)
4. **One trend/season tag** (`#2026finds`, `#fallrecipes`)
5. **Always: `#affiliate`** (compliance)

## Image alt text (yes, Pinterest reads this)

Every pin upload has an "alt text" field. Most people skip it. Use this — it's a free ranking boost AND accessibility win.

**Format:** `[Color] [Product type] in [setting], [key feature]`

Example: `Black 6-quart air fryer on white kitchen counter with crispy fries, digital touchscreen display`

Don't keyword-stuff. One sentence. Pinterest's image AI cross-checks this against the actual image.

## What Pinterest rewards in 2026 (algorithm signals)

In rough order of weight:

1. **Outbound clicks per impression (CTR)** — the #1 signal. Pin titles + images that get clicks get more distribution.
2. **Pin saves** — second most important. A "save" tells Pinterest the pin is reference-worthy.
3. **Domain reputation** — claimed + Rich Pins enabled = boost. Spammy domains get throttled.
4. **Pin freshness** — same image repinned = throttled. New image of same product = treated as fresh.
5. **Long pin descriptions** — 200+ chars outperform short ones (more keyword surface area).
6. **Video pins** — getting heavy distribution preference in 2026. Even a 6-second product loop helps.
7. **Idea Pins (carousel)** — multi-page pins for tutorials/roundups; explicitly favored for "best of" content.

Things that DON'T help:
- Hashtag count above 5
- Generic descriptions ("Cool product! Check it out!")
- Direct affiliate links repeated >3×/day
- Pin text overlay larger than 30% of the image (visual noise penalty)
